# How to Build a Real Edge — Data & Signals Roadmap

## 1. The core problem in one paragraph

Your OHLCV research already proved the hard truth honestly: price-and-volume alone carry **no information the market hasn't already priced**. 1,693 intraday variants and 195 swing variants all died — not because the code was wrong, but because any pattern visible in past prices is visible to everyone, so it gets arbitraged down to a gross edge of ~1-4 bps, which then drowns under the **14 bps intraday cost wall** (and the **29-35 bps delivery wall** for multi-day cash trades). There are only two ways out of this box: bring **different information** the price chart doesn't contain (fundamentals, events, flow, text), or **lower the cost wall** so a small real edge survives (trade slower so a fixed cost amortizes over a bigger move, or trade the future instead of cash so the statutory cost itself shrinks). Everything below is one of those two moves.

## 2. The two levers

**Lever A — New information.** Add a feature the price series cannot contain: an earnings surprise, a superstar investor's disclosed buy, a value/quality factor rank, the textual tone of a concall. This is where genuine, documented anomalies live (PEAD, value/quality, profitability), but it costs data-engineering effort and the data has traps (survivorship, look-ahead, restatement).

**Lever B — Lower the cost wall.** Two cheap structural moves, no new data at all:
- **Longer horizon.** A signal worth 1-4 bps intraday is hopeless, but the *same* signal expressed over weeks rides a 2-5% move that covers a 29 bps cost 5-15x. You already own the data to test this.
- **Futures wrapper.** Trading the stock-*future* instead of cash collapses the round-trip cost from ~29-35 bps to **~9-15 bps** (futures STT is sell-side-only at 0.05%, no DP/demat charge, no buy-leg STT) — and unlocks overnight shorts, which cash equity forbids. This is statutory tax structure, not an anomaly, so it cannot be arbitraged away.

**Why B is the cheapest first experiment:** it requires *zero new data vendors and almost zero new code*. Your harness (`swing_dataset.py`, `swing_probe.py`, `delivery_costs.py`) already builds the daily panel, computes overnight/momentum/low-vol features, and nets cost. Lever B is a horizon-and-cost-model change on data you already paid for — you can run it this week. Lever A is the bigger prize but takes weeks of scraping and careful point-in-time discipline before you can even believe a result.

> ✅ **Verified note:** the research draft claimed `delivery_costs.futures_short_leg_pct()` still used the old 0.02% futures STT — but the code ALREADY uses the correct **0.05% sell-side (eff. 2026-04-01)**. No fix needed. Still: use ~**12-15 bps** (not the 9 bps floor) for a small retail book — lot-size granularity, flat ₹20/leg brokerage, future-leg spread, and monthly roll all push effective cost above the headline.

## 4. A concrete 4-step plan you can start this week

Every step routes through the existing harness and must clear the **same gates that made your no-edge verdict credible**: n≥2000 observations, out-of-sample headline, **PBO < 10%**, and net of *real* cost (`delivery_costs.py`). If it doesn't clear, you kill it — honestly, like you killed OHLCV.

**Step 1 — Patch the cost model (½ day).** Fix `delivery_costs.futures_short_leg_pct()`: STT 0.02% → **0.05%** (effective 2026-04-01), and set the working futures round-trip to **~12-15 bps**, not the 9 bps floor. Add an explicit `overnight_carry` cost helper so the overnight label is charged *one monthly roll*, not full delivery per night. Nothing downstream is trustworthy until this is right.

**Step 2 — Run the two free, zero-data experiments (2-3 days).** On the existing archive, filtered to liquid names and survivorship-flagged:
- **Low-vol long-only:** rank `rvol_20d`, long bottom quantile, `evaluate_swing_signal(horizon=20)` with delivery cost. This is your single best-odds shot and costs nothing.
- **Overnight-via-futures:** `fwd_overnight_1` label, costed as monthly futures carry (Step 1 helper), on F&O names + Nifty/BankNifty futures.

Both go through OOS + PBO. If either survives, you have your first validated edge candidate.

**Step 3 — Build the events dataset and test PEAD (1-2 weeks).** Write an events loader: earnings dates from NSE `corporate-announcements`/`event-calendar`, **anchored to the actual filing timestamp** (after-market filings start drift the next session — respect the no-look-ahead rule already enforced in `swing_dataset.py`). Compute a seasonal-random-walk surprise from free Screener EPS; add FinBERT tone on your RSS archive as a second feature. Attach as point-in-time columns, run the **long-only top-surprise leg** through `swing_probe.py` at the delivery (or futures) wall, restricted to liquid F&O names. Spend the ₹5,900 on Trendlyne consensus *only if* the free version shows a signal.

**Step 4 — Only if Steps 2-3 produce a survivor, start the slow factor book (weeks).** Scrape standardized statements + the real NSE announcement date, forward-fill factors with the announcement-date lag, cross-sectionally rank per day, and run through the swing harness with the cost model **extended to include STT both legs + STCG/LTCG tax** at realistic ~30-40%/yr turnover. Treat every positive result as **provisional until re-validated on a delisting-inclusive source** (borrow a Prowess seat if you possibly can) — your free data is survivorship-biased and will overstate returns by ~5pp/yr.

## 5. Honest expectations

Be clear-eyed: even doing all of this correctly, **retail edges are small, fragile, and decay**. You are not going to find day-trading riches — your own research already closed that door, and the cost walls are arithmetic, not opinion. What you *can* realistically reach is a **small, validated, low-turnover edge**: something like a low-vol or long-only-momentum or PEAD sleeve that, net of real cost and after passing PBO, beats Nifty by a modest margin (think low-single-digit % annualized of alpha, not a Sharpe-3 fantasy) with lower drawdown — held for weeks-to-months, not minutes.

**Success looks like this:** one or two signals that survive OOS + PBO < 10% net of the *correct* cost wall, implemented long-only or via the futures wrapper, rebalanced slowly, sized small, and re-validated on survivorship-clean data before you risk conviction. The binding constraint will have shifted from *transaction cost* to *data quality* (point-in-time, delisting-inclusive) and *tax drag* — which is exactly where a careful individual quant can still compete, because it rewards diligence over speed. The discipline that produced your honest no-edge verdict is the same discipline that will tell you, credibly, when you've actually found something. Most candidates here will fail that test. The goal is to find the one or two that don't.
